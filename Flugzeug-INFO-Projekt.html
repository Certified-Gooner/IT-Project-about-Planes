HISTORY

BC

Humans have dreamed of flight for centuries; mythological stories like Icarus' wings in Greek mythology illustrate this fascination.



1490s

Leonardo da Vinci designs the "Ornithopter," a machine aimed at flight, although it was never built.



Late 1800s

Early experiments with gliders and manned balloons by pioneers like Otto Lilienthal, the Wright brothers, and others paved the way for powered flight



1903

Wright Brothers (Orville and Wilbur Wright) achieve the first controlled, sustained flight of a powered aircraft, the Wright Flyer in Kitty Hawk, North Carolina (12 seconds, 120 feet).



1909

Louis Bl√©riot makes the first successful flight across the English Channel in a monoplane.



1914

The first airmail service begins between the United States and Mexico.



WWI

Airplanes are used in combat for reconnaissance, bombing, and dogfights. Aircraft technology improves rapidly during this period.



1927

Charles Lindbergh completes the first solo nonstop transatlantic flight from New York to Paris in the Spirit of St. Louis.



1930s

Advancements in aircraft design, with the introduction of the DC-3, a reliable passenger plane that revolutionizes commercial aviation.



WWII

Development of faster, more powerful military aircraft, including the B-17 Flying Fortress, Spitfire, P-51 Mustang, and B-29 Superfortress. The jet engine is developed towards the end of the war.



1947

Chuck Yeager breaks the sound barrier in the Bell X-1, marking a major milestone in aviation history.



1950s

Commercial jet airliners, like the Boeing 707, are introduced, transforming international travel by making air travel faster and more accessible.



1969

The Concorde supersonic airliner makes its first flight, offering passengers the ability to fly faster than the speed of sound (Mach 2).



1970

The Boeing 747, the first "jumbo jet," is introduced, revolutionizing air travel by allowing for large-scale, long-haul flights.



1980s-90s

Advancements in materials, fuel efficiency, and avionics make airplanes safer, more cost-effective, and environmentally friendly.



2000s

The Airbus A380, the world's largest passenger airliner, enters service, offering unprecedented seating capacity.



2010s

The Boeing 787 Dreamliner and Airbus A350 introduce new fuel-efficient technologies, lighter materials, and improved passenger comfort.



Present day

Ongoing developments in electric and hybrid aircraft, as well as drones, are paving the way for the future of aviation, including more sustainable and automated technologies.



Future

Research into urban air mobility (flying cars, VTOLs), hypersonic flight, and space tourism are shaping the next frontiers of aviation.
